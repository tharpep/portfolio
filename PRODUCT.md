# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Engineering hiring managers, recruiters, and fellow engineers evaluating Pryce Tharpe for full-time software roles. They arrive mid-skim on a laptop, often from a LinkedIn or GitHub link, deciding in under a minute whether this person is technically sharp and has taste. A smaller second audience: peers and collaborators who land here from the photography site or a project repo.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that makes the case for Pryce as an engineer who ships real systems (AI agents, API gateways, RAG pipelines, cloud infra) and has genuine craft. Success is a visitor leaving with a clear, specific sense of what he builds and a reason to reach out, not a generic "nice site" impression. The site itself is a work sample: its build quality is part of the argument.

## Brand Personality

Precise, curious, engineered. The voice is plainspoken and specific, an engineer describing what he actually built, not a marketer. Confidence without inflation. Warmth comes through in the personal details (photography, guitar, building PCs) but the spine is technical rigor. Three words: exact, curious, made-by-hand.

## Anti-references

- The dark-mode cyan/blue gradient developer portfolio (what this site used to be): gradient clip-text headings, neon-on-charcoal, identical rounded card grids, mono font on everything as a "technical" costume. This is the saturated AI-dev-portfolio look and the single thing to get away from.
- SaaS hero-metric templates and "I build seamless, scalable solutions" marketing copy.
- The editorial-magazine serif lane (display serif + italic + ruled columns). The photography section already owns a tasteful version of editorial; the dev site must read as a different, engineered world, not a second magazine.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is a work sample.** Every detail (contrast, motion, responsive behavior, copy) is evidence of how he builds. Sloppiness here contradicts the claim.
2. **Signal over noise.** Generous space, strong hierarchy, one decisive accent. A recruiter skimming should grasp the shape in seconds.
3. **Show the systems.** Lead with what was actually built and the measurable result, not adjectives. Specifics ("3 hours to under 10 minutes", "20+ tools", "RTX 3080") carry the weight.
4. **Engineered, not decorated.** Precision, alignment, and a technical/draftsman vocabulary (rules, indices, mono metadata) instead of decorative gradients and glows.
5. **Two worlds, one person.** The dev site and the photography site are deliberately distinct aesthetics that cross-link; the connection is the human, not a shared template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, verified against the off-white paper (no light-gray-on-white drift). Full keyboard support with visible focus rings, skip link, semantic landmarks, and `aria-current` on nav. Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` path, and all content is visible without JavaScript (motion enhances an already-rendered default).
